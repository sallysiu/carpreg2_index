CARPREG II Risk Prediction Index

Determining Risk of Maternal Cardiac Complications in Pregnant Women

While the risk of maternal mortality in pregnant women with heart disease is rare, cardiac complications can occur and the risk of complications can be estimated by integrating general cardiac risk predictors, lesion-specific risk information, and factors related to process of care.

1. Prior cardiac events or arrhythmias
2. Baseline NYHA II-IV cyanosis
3. Mechanical valve
4. Ventricular dysfunction
5. High risk left-sided valve disease/left ventricular outflow tract obstruction
6. Pulmonary hypertension
7. Coronary artery disease
8. High risk aortopathy
9. No prior cardiac intervention
10. Late pregnancy assessment
