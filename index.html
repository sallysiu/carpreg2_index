Determining Risk of Maternal Cardiac Complications in Pregnant Women

Prediction of maternal cardiac complications in women with heart disease requires integration of clinical information, echocardiographic parameters, the specific maternal cardiac lesion, and process of care variables.

9. No prior cardiac intervention

While the risk of maternal mortality in pregnant women with heart disease is rare, cardiac complications can occur and the risk of complications can be estimated by integrating general cardiac risk predictors, lesion-specific risk information, and factors related to process of care. Careful cardiovascular clinical assessment remains teh foundation of risk stratification of pregnant women with heart disease.
